## Star ID plan — the moon-line / brightness-pattern method (Peter's idea)

Goal: identify stars + estimate magnitudes from the in-focus full-res
frames. The repo ALREADY has the pipeline (bin/detect-stars,
solve-detections, cross-match-gaia, photo-compare; solve-field 0.97 +
Tycho-2 index files; photutils 3.0). But blind plate-solving a 102deg
distorted field is risky, and tonight blind prediction from the moon
boresight found no star (foreground lights dominate detection).

**The method to build (tomorrow, on previous nights):** the camera is
FIXED, so every pixel maps to a fixed az/alt. The 7-point moon track
gives the pixel curve of a known sky locus. Render that as a GREEN LINE
across a night frame; Peter `p`-marks where stars cross it; the
sequence of brightnesses along the line (bright-dim-dim-bright-dim...) is
a near-unique fingerprint matched against the catalog stars on that locus
-> identification, then flux->magnitude via their catalog mags. No
precise pointing needed, just the moon line + the brightness pattern.

**Moon line (full-res pixel space):** y = 2.11e-5*x^2 + 0.0425*x + 1195.7
(fit through the 7 moon points, residual 1.2px). Measured x 186-1355;
smoothly extrapolated across the frame. Rendered green on the 22:59:59
frame (~/tmp/moon-line-2259.png). NB the moon at alt~30 sits LOW on this
tilted camera (the line runs through the lower sky near the rooftop
murk), and TONIGHT was hazy -> few crisp stars on the line. Method is
sound; needs a clear night with stars along the line.

**Tomorrow:** develop the methodology on PREVIOUS clear nights (06-21
in-focus binned; 06-22 in-focus full-res). The moon line transfers
between nights (camera fixed = same az/alt->pixel), so a clear archived
night can be marked even though its own moon wasn't tracked. Build:
render-line tool, mark, predict the catalog brightness pattern on the

**THE STRATEGIC FRAME (Peter's key idea): the moon is a monthly NET.**
Every clear night the moon traces a DIFFERENT track across the sensor
(its declination swings -29 to +25deg over a lunar month; the track's
az/alt sweep changes nightly). Each track is a curve of PRECISELY-KNOWN
sky positions (ephemeris). Accumulate them over a month (and across
months) and they form a dense NET of known-position curves covering the
whole field. Why this is powerful:
  - Tracks at different declinations CROSS at angles -> non-parallel
    known curves break the single-arc degeneracy that gave us the wrong
    plate scale (one arc can't separate az/alt sensitivity; many can).
    -> pins pointing + plate scale + k1,k2 distortion across the field.
  - Any star near any track gets caught in the net -> a cumulative
    catalog of identified stars at known pixels -> a full astrometric +
    photometric solution FROM THE MOON ALONE, no plate-solving.
  - Free, precise, self-scanning: just capture every clear night; the
    moon re-scans a new strip automatically.
Scheduling nuance: the moon is in v3w's field at a dark/usable hour only
in BURSTS (~first week of this cycle, 06-22..06-28, dec -9..-29; then it
shifts to daytime/other azimuth). So the net fills in bursts, densifying
over multiple lunar months. Capture-every-clear-night is the policy.

## Raw Bayer: use full-res for DETECTION, never warp the mosaic

Capture chain clarified (two binning stages, easy to conflate):
- on-chip at capture: was 2x2-binned readout (sensor_size 2304x1296);
  from 2026-06-22 it's FULL native 4608x2592 (no on-chip bin). NEVER 4x4.
- in software: nightly-cam/detrans/sweeps then do ANOTHER 2x2 bin
  (bin2x2 -> grey). So final processed products were 1152x648 (old) /
  2304x1296 (now). The stored FITS is RAW BAYER (RGGB, 2D mosaic) at the
  capture resolution -> full res + colour are preserved in the archive.

**The governing rule (Peter): Bayer is great for star extraction but
CANNOT be transformed naively.** Any geometric op (undistort, derotate,
de-translate, detrans registration) interpolates, and interpolating a
raw mosaic mixes adjacent R/G/B -> the moire we saw in the full-res deep
still. So:
  - DETECTION / astrometry / photometry (NO transform): use the raw
    Bayer at full res. Star-finding is centroids + flux on the as-captured
    grid, no resampling. The GREEN plane is best — full pixel grid, no
    phase mixing, and lower noise (measured: green-plane sky noise 1960
    vs grey-bin 5684 on a v3w frame -> better faint-star SNR). This is
    exactly the moon-net star-ID task (detect where stars cross the moon
    line = no transform) -> run it on full-res green, catch fainter stars.
  - STACKING / registration (NEEDS transform): never warp the mosaic.
    Split RGGB -> 4 complete grids (or demosaic) -> transform each plane
    independently -> recombine. Per-plane, then combine.

Experiment Peter wants to run (his): does full-res Bayer green-plane beat
grey-bin for star extraction (1), save disk (2: archive mosaic ~10MB Rice
vs storing a derived plane), save compute (3: skip FITS->binned->stackable),
keep options (4: mosaic is most flexible -> archive it). Data's all in the
FITS; this is his to run.
