## Trail line-filter for the diff sweep (NEW, work-in-progress)

The diff sweep shows star trails amid two unwanted classes that make the mp4
noisy + high-bandwidth: **dense single-pixel dots** (noise/hot pixels) and a
**fuzzy non-linear cloud** (glow). Goal: keep the linear trails, suppress the
rest.

**Approach** (`bin/diff-sweep --line-filter`, flag-gated, default OFF):
per-pixel oriented morphological open ALONG the trail direction minus ACROSS
it. Trails survive (bright along, dark across); dots erased (smaller than the
kernel); cloud cancels (isotropic → along≈across).

**The good idea (Peter's hint): orientation is ANALYTIC, not estimated.** The
trails are co-oriented locally but curve across the frame from barrel
distortion. `trail_angle_field()` computes the per-pixel trail direction from
camera.json (k1,k2 + detrans 7.8° motion angle) — the inverse-Jacobian of the
undistort map applied to the motion vector. Validated clean: centre 7.8°,
corners ~53/109°, smooth. Far more robust than a structure-tensor estimate
(which falsely fires on cloud edges).

**Status: works in principle, NOT tuned.** On the crops it was great (dots
34062→~70px, cloud 21%→~4%). On a real full 06-24 frame:
- plain diff frame 1.26 MB → filtered 35 KB (**~26× smaller** — the dots were
  the entropy). Huge bandwidth win.
- BUT the in-pipeline run **over-suppressed** — nearly all black, one trail
  left. The threshold operates on the raw `max−mean` float (different scale
  than the 0-255 jpg the crop tuning used), so the fixed thresh=25 was wrong.
- Retuned on the rendered frame (thresh≈4) → trails preserved, noise cut, but
  residual foreground/edge texture remains.

Conclusion: the algorithm + analytic field are sound; the **threshold needs
to be noise-relative (per-frame), not absolute**, and the foreground wants
masking. Next: an interactive splay tuning plugin (see TODO) before defaulting
it on. Examples left at `~/tmp/linefilter-test/` (plain / over-suppressed /
tune-thr4). Design: `design/trail-line-filter.md`.
