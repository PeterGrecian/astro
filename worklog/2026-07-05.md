## Geometry corrections (Peter drove these)

- **The image is a SPHERE segment, not a plane.** The flat-plane rotation fit's
  runaway pixel-pole (y→20000) was the model failing — sidereal rotation on a
  102° sphere-segment doesn't project to rotation-about-a-pixel. Correct model:
  work on the sphere (az/alt motion is pure physics from latitude), fit only the
  camera PROJECTION (boresight/focal/k1k2). Pole is an az/alt axis, not a pixel.
- **Why v3w needs the FOV scale but astrocam doesn't:** astrocam has the pole
  IN-frame → arcs reveal their radius → scale self-calibrates. v3w's pole is
  off-frame → flat arcs → scale/pole degenerate → must supply FOV scale.
- **drift(x,y) = known sidereal + lens distortion (+ refraction).** The vector
  field is the RESIDUAL — the correction, not the whole motion. Because the
  camera is fixed, each pixel always sees the same altitude, so refraction is a
  static function of (x,y) — the field captures lens + atmosphere with no time
  term. Refraction is separable as the pressure-dependent departure from
  rotational symmetry (a 20-day + barometer campaign could detect it).

## Sub-pixel astrometry from the streak, and the mosaic

- **PSF is ~1 px, UNDERSAMPLED** (peak >> neighbours). Measured on the MOSAIC
  first (wrong — my error), then: interpolation BLURS the sharp point (2.1 px);
  for white sources the **gain-corrected mosaic is the sharpest data** (uses
  only real samples). Peter: normalise each streak to its known catalog colour,
  not just white → the residual imbalance is the **sensor colour map**. The
  irreducible checkerboard after white-balance IS the sub-pixel aliasing =
  drizzle signal.
- **The streak is a precision gift.** Fitting a line through the ~22 cross-streak
  centroids pins the path to **0.14 px** (11 arcsec from one 55 s frame). Two
  adjacent streaks TILE end-to-end; individual line fits agree to 0.04°, but the
  COMBINED fit reveals the drift ARC curvature (the vector field). Peter's
  refinement: the streak length is known LOCALLY (smooth distortion) → use it as
  a matched-filter template → the optimal FAINT detector (how to find Neptune).

## Super-resolution — drizzle + focus-breathing dither (the cool one)

- **Drizzle** (Fruchter & Hook): shrink each pixel to a "drop", rain it onto a
  finer grid at its sub-pixel position; many dithered frames super-resolve.
  Prototype confirmed cross-drift super-res (0.5 px FWHM) but showed the
  reconstruction is **orientation-dependent** (sharp along drift, jitter-limited
  across).
- **Peter's design: focus BREATHING as a commanded RADIAL dither.** Stepping the
  VCM focus changes magnification → a radial shift ∝ radius = a deterministic
  dither in a NEW axis, complementary to the sky-drift, strongest at the edges
  (where drift is weak and distortion worst). One focus sweep ALSO optimises
  focus (PSF vs dioptre) — the crudely-pinned 3.15 was never verified.
- **MEASURED live on v3w (daytime):** breathing **~0.28 %/dioptre** (∝R fits);
  1 dioptre = ~4 px @ mid-radius, ~7 px @ edge. Daytime sharpness peaks at
  lp≈6.0 (rooftops, not infinity — but the sweep-finds-focus mechanism works).
  VCM mechanical step ~0.5 dioptre = ~0.35 px @ mid-R (API accepts 0.02 but the
  coil is quantised coarser; reported ≠ actual). Turns the VCM from a liability
  (jitter) into an active micro-scanning stage — piezo dithering, free in
  software.
