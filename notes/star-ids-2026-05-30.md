## Camera orientation (corrected)

Earlier hypothesis was wrong. Confirmed orientation:

- **Zenith is at the BOTTOM** of the image (cut off by the eaves of the house).
- **Pole is at TOP-RIGHT** of the image.
- **East is on the LEFT**, west on the RIGHT (sky stars at bottom-left drift
  down-and-right with time, which is north-and-west; west is right confirmed).
- The camera points roughly north, tilted about 20° east, with the sensor
  rotated about its optical axis (significant rotation, see below).
- A5 (image bottom-left) is therefore looking up toward the Big Dipper
  handle area, NW sky — NOT east-of-zenith as I initially guessed.

These bright stars were predicted off-image (or below horizon at obs time):

- **Capella** (α Aur): alt 7.5°, az 1.8° — below the local visible horizon
  (eaves/buildings to the north block view down to that altitude).
  Was my earlier wrong identification for A5 #1.
- **Vega** (α Lyr): alt 72.6°, az 129.6° — in the SE sky, NOT in our field
  which looks NW.
- **Deneb**, **Eltanin**, **Mirfak**, **Algol**, **Cassiopeia** — all
  predicted at pixel positions outside the 1296×972 frame.

## Implications for the WCS work

- The "single global TAN+SIP" WCS isn't going to fit a 56°-wide field
  with the pole IN the frame. Need a polar projection.
- Per-tile local poles are the right unit: each captures local distortion.
- The mapping from (image x, y) to (RA, Dec) is:
  ```
  r_pix = hypot(x − pole_x, y − pole_y)        # distance from true NCP pixel
  θ_pix = atan2(y − pole_y, x − pole_x)        # image angle
  pole_d_deg = F(r_pix)                         # radial function — captures distortion
  az = θ_pix − θ_rot                            # azimuth on sky
  (alt, az) → (RA, Dec) via standard transform
  ```
  Six parameters: (pole_x, pole_y), θ_rot, and three radial coefficients
  for F. We've measured all four of (pole_x, pole_y, θ_rot, F at one
  radius), so the remaining freedom is two extra polynomial coefficients
  for F's curvature.

## Photometric zeropoint (corrected)

With A5 #1 = Alioth (V=1.76) and flux 5,138,531:

```
zp = V + 2.5·log10(flux) = 1.76 + 16.78 = 18.54
```

Previous estimate using "Capella" was 16.86 — wrong by ~1.7 mag because
Capella was misidentified. Real zeropoint is **~18.54 mag** for this
